32 states in the country have legislation against boycotting Israel, targeting advocates for Palestinian rights

This data and more detailed information about state and federal legislation targeting advocacy for Palestinian rights is from Palestine Legal's legislation tracking project.

The majority of anti-boycott legislation is against state or local governments having contracts with anyone who supports a boycott of Israel. Though the specifics vary state-to-state, it usually means contractors — from public school teachers to residents applying for aid after Hurricane Harvey — have to sign an agreement not to boycott products from Isrel or illegeal Israeli settlements. Some legislation also involve creating a blacklist of companies and individuals the state is banned from working with.

Legislation against state's investments bars the state from investing in companies that support a boycott of Israel. This includes how public univerisites invest funds and the state managed pensions.

Bills that involves redefining antisemitism are different from specifically targetting those who support a boycott of Israel, but similar in the attempt to stifle advocacy for Palestinian rights and criticism of Israel. They involve the state adopting a highly contested definition of antisemitism that includes broader criticism of Israel. This definition was signed into federal law through an executive order in 2019, despite a main author of the bill speaking against its adoption in government and use to silence Palestinian advocacy.
